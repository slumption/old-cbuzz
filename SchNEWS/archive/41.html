NUN - SENSE

Congregation of the Poor Servants of the Mother of God
vs
Kestral, Alex and persons unknown

Royal Courts of Justice, The Strand, London Thurs Sept 28th @ 2pm

It promises to be one of the most bizarre court cases of the year - and potentially a test-case for the new 24hrs-to-get-out or face jail laws under the Criminal Justice Act.

A community of travellers and homeless people moved into St. Annes Convent, a disused 100-room Nunnery, and have set up a creche, a gallery, a meditation room, rehearsal spaces and meeting rooms for a host of local groups. But hopes to turn it into Britain's biggest commune has been thwarted by a Order of Catholic nuns who are taking the squatters to the High Court.

Many local people have joined the fight. Donations of food, furniture, clothing, toys and money have been coming in from neighbours in Hove (actually). Even the local rag is praising the initiative as "well organised" and "effective". This summer the squatters had been forcefully evicted from seafront arches and had launched 'Operation Crusty' to commandeer the derelict West Pier to highlight their plight. High-profile occupations of the Milkmaid Pavilion, the Putting Green and Bedford Square were shortlived before the move to St Anne's Convent.

However, the sisters, known as the Poor Servants of the Mother of God, who have recently sold property to boxer Chris Eubank, have issued the summons for repossession. And one local rent-a-gob councillor has even called on nearby schoolgirls to mount "a St Trinian's style attack brandishing hockey sticks" to get them out.

THE COURTHOUSE - ONE YEAR ON

The Courthouse, which was opened last year by Justice? as a squatted community centre in Brighton, is falling down. Our on-the-spot SchNEWS reporter discovered that the US owners, Alford Developments, have left the 100-year-old former magistrates court to rot. A third of the building is flooded with severe water damage.

On Tuesday, the day of the anniversary, extra security were drafted in and a police van stood nearby - some officers checking out under cars for bombs! It seems the planning application to demolish the Grade II listed building and build more offices and car parks (decision postponed to Oct 10th) - even if rejected - could be pointless. Only English Heritage have a legal right to enter the building - let's hope they prosecute for criminal neglect.

COOLTAN MOVES ON

Sadness and melancholy thoughts all round as London's most vibrant and celebrated squat, CoolTan, move out after three years this Sunday. Named after a sun cream lotion, the Brixton Collective housed the Freedom Network - who got much of the anti-CJA ball rolling - and a million wonderful projects in the former dole office where John Major used to sign on.

The Voice newspaper move in from next door on Monday and may well find some party heads emerging into the newsroom days later after Saturdays final blow-out bash (" 'scuse me, d'ya know where the bogs are mate"). Go pay your respects if you are in the area. SchNEWS sends warm wishes and best hopes to all who've been involved. Watch out for Greenland!

SQUATTERS RE-OPEN ABANDONED CINEMA

With headlines like 'Carry on Squatting' you could be forgiven for thinking you we're reading The SchNEWS but believe it not these were the words from the Camden Journal! Squatters moved in to the popular art-deco gem in the centre of Camden Town four weeks ago. The building has been empty for the past three years since it was acquired by a property subsiduary of Lohnro and is now falling into disrepair due to neglect.

A "person unknown" told Schnews: "We have won the support of the local council, community, traders, taxi firms, fire brigade -even the police !"

The squatters are also in dialogue with the 'Friends of Parkway' lobby group, which is chaired by Michael "Monty Python" Palin. In fact, support has been so strong that the local bobby even wished the occupier and persons unknown good luck on their way to the latest court case.

And if the owners succeed in evicting everybody? A Planning Officer has assured the squatters the cinema will stand empty for another four years, with possible demolition after that. The next court case is Friday 29th. Meanwhile, enjoy all-night cinema and ambient chill-out cafe every Sat.

Penalty!

Footie Fans have made a bold bid to knock the Hunt Sabs off their long running number one spot on the Arrestometer. SchNEWS has learned that since the CJA came into force in November Liverpool Police have arrested 68 people under Section 166. This clause makes it a criminal offence to sell on a match ticket, even if its at cost price! . Of those arrested 49 were prosecuted. Mike from Football Fans Against the CJA told SchNEWS " arrests in football have been going down for some time - it seems only under the CJA is there a growth area."

...and Finally

Q. "So you don't want to see my bottom again Wiggy," shouted Annarita Muraglia at bemused Judge Andrew Brooks, as she turned her back on him in the dock, hitched up her red floral skirt, and displayed her naked buttocks to him in Snaresbrook Crown Court.

The outburst from Muraglia, 24, occurred while the judge was concluding his summary of her previous offences: "In December last year, you flashed your bottom, which bore the message Merry Christmas at a high court judge. You have bashed one district judge, thrown a telephone at another and at a preliminary hearing earlier this year, you rushed towards the bench and for reasons best known to yourself, proceeded to spray me with green paint. Some of it is still visible on my wig and my robe, despite my wife's best endeavours. On that occassion I gave you a chance to purge contempt, but you have shown no remorse whatsoever and even wrote to me boasting of your previous contempts. I am now satisfied that your behaviour was calculated to interfere with the administration of justice."

After the incident, Judge Brooks asked officials "What did she have written on her bottom? Happy something I think." The complete message was subsequently discovered to be Happy Birthday. Muraglia was sentenced to 15 months in jail for contempt.
